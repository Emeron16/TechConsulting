# Installing a Printer Driver

TechConsulting's approved office printers (HP LaserJet Enterprise M610 and Xerox VersaLink C405) require a driver to be installed locally before they can be added. Drivers are pre-staged in the internal driver store so employees don't need to download anything from the internet.

## When you need this

You need to install a printer driver before mapping a new printer for the first time on a given laptop or desktop, or if your existing printer driver has become corrupted and print jobs are failing or printing garbled output.

## Steps (Windows)

1. Confirm the exact printer model and its display name from your floor plan or by asking your office manager (for example, `HP-LaserJet-3F-East`).
2. Open **Settings > Bluetooth & devices > Printers & scanners**.
3. Click **Add device** and allow Windows to search — if the driver is already staged, it will often install automatically once the printer is found on the network.
4. If it is not found automatically, the driver must be added from the approved internal driver store using the printer's registered driver name (IT staff or the automated assistant can do this for you).
5. Once installed, proceed to the "Connecting to a Network Printer" article to add the specific printer queue.

## Notes

TechConsulting does not permit installing printer drivers downloaded directly from a manufacturer's website, since these are not verified against our security baseline. Only drivers present in the internal approved driver store may be installed. If your printer model isn't in the store, submit a request via the TC Helpdesk Portal.
